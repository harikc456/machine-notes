Do Transformers Need Three Projections? Systematic Study of QKV Variants
Ali Kayyam, Anusha Madan Gopal, M Anthony Lewis — BrainChip Inc.
ICML 2026, arXiv:2606.04032

Three projection-sharing variants evaluated across synthetic tasks, vision, and LM (300M and 1.2B params, 10B tokens):
- Q=K-V: unified Q and K, separate V. Symmetric attention maps. Adds 2D pos enc to handle symmetry for non-causal tasks.
- Q-K=V: separate Q, unified K and V. Key finding: 50% KV cache reduction, only +3.1% PPL at 300M (improves to +2.48% at 1.2B).
- Q=K=V: single projection for all three. Catastrophic: +25.4% PPL.

Why Q-K=V works: K and V share representational space (cosine sim 0.73 across layers, similar effective rank 687 vs 702/1024). K is rich enough to absorb V's role during training.
Why Q=K-V fails: forces symmetric QKᵀ attention matrix, breaking causal directionality. +4.9% PPL with zero KV cache benefit.
Why Q=K=V fails: combines both pathologies.

Compound with head sharing (orthogonal axes):
- Q-GQA-4: 87.5% cache reduction, +3.9% PPL
- Q-MQA: 96.9% cache reduction, +4.8% PPL — near-theoretical maximum

Scale: rankings stable at 1.2B/10B. Degradation improves with scale (3.1% → 2.48% for Q-K=V).
Deployment: Q-K=V is the recommended variant for production; Q=K-V should be avoided despite competitive training loss.
