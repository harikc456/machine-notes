# TurboQuant: Online Vector Quantization with Near-optimal Distortion Rate

**Authors:** Amir Zandieh, Majid Daliri, Majid Hadian, Vahab Mirrokni  
**Affiliation:** Google Research, New York University, Google DeepMind  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, April 2025  
**arXiv:** 2504.19874

## Summary

TurboQuant is a near-optimal online vector quantization algorithm for high-dimensional vectors (notably KV cache embeddings). It achieves near-optimal distortion bounds in both MSE and inner product preservation across all bit-widths and dimensions, via random rotation + MSE-optimal scalar quantization + 1-bit QJL residual.

## Core Insight

MSE-optimal scalar quantizers do not produce unbiased inner product estimators — they introduce bias. TurboQuant addresses this with a two-stage pipeline:
1. **MSE quantizer:** Random rotation (induces near-independent Beta-distributed coordinates) → apply per-coordinate MSE-optimal scalar quantizer
2. **1-bit QJL residual:** Apply 1-bit Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss transform to the residual → unbiased inner product estimator

This combination achieves near-optimal distortion-rate bounds (within a constant factor ≈ 2.7) provably.

## Properties

- **Data-oblivious:** No calibration data needed; online application
- **Accelerator-friendly:** Random rotation + scalar quantization are GPU-efficient
- **Proven near-optimal:** Formal proof against Shannon distortion-rate lower bounds
- **KV cache:** Quality neutral at 3.5 bits/channel; marginal degradation at 2.5 bits/channel
- **Nearest-neighbor search:** Outperforms product quantization in recall while reducing indexing time to near-zero
