# H₂O: Heavy-Hitter Oracle for Efficient Generative Inference of Large Language Models

**Authors:** Zhenyu Zhang, Ying Sheng, Tianyi Zhou, Tianlong Chen, Lianmin Zheng, Ruisi Cai, Zhao Song, Yuandong Tian, Christopher Ré, Clark Barrett, Zhangyang Wang, Beidi Chen  
**Affiliation:** UT Austin, UC Berkeley, CMU, Stanford, Meta AI, and others  
**Venue:** NeurIPS 2023  
**arXiv:** 2306.14048

## Summary

H₂O identifies a "heavy-hitter" phenomenon in attention: a small fraction (~20%) of tokens accumulate disproportionately large attention scores across heads and layers. Retaining only these heavy hitters plus recent tokens in the KV cache achieves near-lossless inference with drastically reduced cache size.

## Core Observation

Analyzing attention score distributions across LLMs (OPT, LLaMA, GPT-NeoX), ~20% of tokens consistently receive >80% of total attention mass. These "heavy hitters" are stable across layers and heads. Evicting non-heavy-hitter tokens from the KV cache causes minimal quality degradation.

## H₂O Algorithm

- Maintain a KV budget of size `k`
- Track cumulative attention score sums per token position
- At each decoding step, retain the top-`k₁` heavy hitters (by cumulative score) + `k₂` most recent tokens; evict the rest
- `k₁ + k₂ = k`; hyperparameters tuned per model/task

## Results

- **29× throughput increase** on OPT-6.7B vs. full-KV inference at matched quality
- **1.9× latency reduction** in generation
- Perplexity within 0.1–0.5 ppt of full-KV on WikiText-2 and other benchmarks
- Works without retraining or fine-tuning

## Limitations

- Requires dynamic score tracking (minor overhead per step)
- Heavy-hitter identity shifts across tasks — may need task-specific budget tuning
- Does not address prefill memory; only decode KV cache
