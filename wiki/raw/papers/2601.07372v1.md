# Stronger Normalization-Free Transformers

**Authors:** Mingzhi Chen, Taiming Lu, Jiachen Zhu, Mingjie Sun, Zhuang Liu  
**Affiliation:** Princeton University, New York University, Carnegie Mellon University  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, March 2026  
**arXiv:** 2601.07372

## Summary

Identifies the properties that make point-wise functions effective replacements for normalization layers (LayerNorm), and introduces **Derf** (Dynamic erf) — `erf(αx + s)` — as the strongest such function, outperforming LayerNorm, RMSNorm, and DyT across visual, speech, and DNA modeling.

## Context

DyT (Dynamic Tanh, Zhu et al. 2025) established that point-wise functions can replace normalization layers without accuracy loss. This work asks: can they *surpass* normalization?

## Key Properties Analysis

Studies four properties for normalization-free functions:
1. **Zero-centeredness** — centered around zero
2. **Boundedness** — output values are bounded
3. **Center sensitivity** — sensitive to values near zero
4. **Monotonicity** — monotonically increasing

Identifies which combination is most effective for training stability and final performance.

## Derf: `erf(αx + s)`

- `erf(x)` is the rescaled Gaussian CDF — S-shaped, bounded ∈ (-1, 1)
- α and s are learnable scalars (per-channel or shared)
- The shift `s` allows breaking strict symmetry around zero
- Compared to DyT (`tanh(αx)`): similar shape but with stronger theoretical grounding

## Results

| Method | ViT ImageNet acc | DiT FID | DNA acc |
|---|---|---|---|
| LayerNorm | 82.3% | 45.91 | 86.9% |
| DyT | 82.5% | 45.46 | 86.9% |
| **Derf** | **82.8%** | **43.94** | **87.3%** |

- Consistent wins across domains (vision classification, image generation, speech, DNA)
- Analysis: Derf's gains come from stronger generalization (higher training loss vs. LN in eval mode), not better fitting
