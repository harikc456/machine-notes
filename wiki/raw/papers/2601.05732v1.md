# mHC-lite: You Don't Need 20 Sinkhorn-Knopp Iterations

**Authors:** Yongyi Yang, Jianyang Gao  
**Affiliation:** University of Michigan (CSE; CBS-NTT Physics of AI), Harvard University, NTT Research Inc., Nanyang Technological University  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, January 2026  
**arXiv:** 2601.05732

## Summary

mHC-lite proposes replacing mHC's iterative Sinkhorn-Knopp (SK) normalization with a simple reparameterization that explicitly constructs doubly stochastic matrices as **convex combinations of permutation matrices** (Birkhoff-von Neumann theorem). This guarantees exact doubly stochasticity by construction, using only native matrix operations.

## Problems with mHC's SK Approach

1. **Approximation gap:** Finite SK iterations don't guarantee exact doubly stochasticity; demonstrated that 20 iterations can leave column sums far from 1 (e.g., 1.92, 0.59, 0.59 for a simple 3×3 matrix)
2. **Engineering barrier:** Efficient SK requires specialized fused CUDA kernels; reduces portability; forward-pass stores n! per-iteration intermediate tensors for backward pass

## mHC-lite Reparameterization

By the Birkhoff-von Neumann theorem, any doubly stochastic matrix is a convex combination of permutation matrices:
    H^res = Σᵢ αᵢ Pᵢ  where αᵢ ≥ 0, Σαᵢ = 1

For n=8 streams: stores n! = 40,320 permutation matrices (feasible for small n; but scales as n!).

Key properties:
- Exact doubly stochasticity by construction
- Implementable with native matrix multiplications (no custom kernels)
- Linear and Softmax operations suffice for parameterizing αᵢ

## Results

- Matches or exceeds mHC performance in downstream tasks
- Higher training throughput (naive implementation, no custom kernels)
- Eliminates instabilities observed in both HC and mHC
- Still has factorial parameter complexity O(C·n!) for large n (addressed by KromHC)
