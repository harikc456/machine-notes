# SARATHI: Efficient LLM Inference by Piggybacking Decodes with Chunked Prefills

**Authors:** Amey Agrawal, Ashish Panwar, Jayashree Mohan, Nipun Kwatra, Bhargav S. Gulavani, Ramachandran Ramjee  
**Affiliation:** Microsoft Research India; Georgia Institute of Technology  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, August 2023  
**arXiv:** 2308.16369

## Summary

SARATHI addresses two LLM inference inefficiencies: (1) decode phases have very low GPU utilization at small batch sizes, and (2) varying prefill and decode lengths cause pipeline-parallel micro-batch bubbles. It introduces **chunked-prefills** and **decode-maximal batching**.

## Core Techniques

### Chunked Prefills
Splits a single prefill request into equal compute-sized chunks. Each chunk becomes one iteration unit rather than one complete prefill. This:
- Enables constructing multiple batches from a single prefill request
- Makes batch compute uniform (prefill chunk + decode slots = uniform compute)
- Allows decodes to "piggyback" on prefill chunks without blocking

### Decode-Maximal Batching
Fills remaining batch slots after one prefill chunk with as many decode requests as possible. This:
- Saturates GPU compute during the high-compute prefill chunk
- Gives decode requests a "free ride" (near-zero marginal compute cost)
- Decodes that would otherwise wait in a decode-only batch are handled simultaneously

## Results

- **LLaMA-13B on A6000:** 10× decode throughput, 1.33× end-to-end throughput
- **LLaMA-33B on A100:** 4.25× decode throughput, 1.25× end-to-end throughput
- **Pipeline parallelism (GPT-3, 8-way):** Reduces bubbles by 6.29×, 1.91× end-to-end speedup
