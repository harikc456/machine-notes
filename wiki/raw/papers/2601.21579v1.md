# KromHC: Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections with Kronecker-Product Residual Matrices

**Authors:** Wuyang Zhou, Yuxuan Gu, Giorgos Iacovides, Danilo Mandic  
**Affiliation:** Imperial College London  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, January 2026  
**arXiv:** 2601.21579

## Summary

KromHC constructs HC residual matrices as Kronecker products of smaller (2×2) doubly stochastic matrices. This simultaneously achieves: (1) exact doubly stochasticity, (2) parameter efficiency O(n²C), and (3) PyTorch-native implementation — addressing limitations of both mHC and mHC-lite.

## Motivation

| Criterion | mHC | mHC-lite | KromHC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exact doubly stochastic | ❌ (approx) | ✅ | ✅ |
| Parameter efficient | ❌ O(n³C) | ❌ O(C·n!) | ✅ O(n²C) |
| PyTorch native | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |

## Method: Kronecker Product Parameterization

For residual stream width n = 2^K:
- Parameterize with K independent 2×2 doubly stochastic matrices {P_k}
- Residual matrix = P_1 ⊗ P_2 ⊗ ... ⊗ P_K (Kronecker product)
- Doubly stochastic matrices are closed under Kronecker products → product is doubly stochastic
- Parameter count: K · 2 = log₂(n) · 2 scalars per layer (vs n² for mHC or n! for mHC-lite)

Each 2×2 factor is parameterized as a convex combination of the two 2×2 permutation matrices: `[[a,1-a],[1-a,a]]`.

## Results

- Matches or outperforms mHC and mHC-lite on downstream tasks
- Significantly fewer trainable parameters than both
- No specialized CUDA kernels required
- Stable training (exact doubly stochasticity eliminates gradient explosion)
