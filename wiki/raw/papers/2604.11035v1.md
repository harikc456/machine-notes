# Introspective Diffusion Language Models

**Authors:** Yifan Yu, Yuqing Jian, Junxiong Wang, Zhongzhu Zhou, Donglin Zhuang, Xinyu Fang, Sri Yanamandra, Qingyang Wu, Tri Dao, Xiaoxia Wu, Shuaiwen Leon Song, Ben Athiwaratkun, James Zou, Fan Lai, Chenfeng Xu  
**Affiliation:** Together AI, UIUC, UT Austin, Princeton University, Stanford University  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, April 2026  
**arXiv:** 2604.11035

## Summary

Introduces Introspective Diffusion Language Models (I-DLM), a paradigm that converts pretrained AR models into DLMs while preserving introspective consistency. The key insight: AR models are trained to agree with their own generations (causal masking + logit shifting enforces this), while standard DLMs are not. I-DLM is the first DLM to match same-scale AR quality.

## Core Concept: Introspective Consistency

**Introspective acceptance rate α:** measures whether a model internally accepts its previously generated tokens — specifically, `α = (1/L) Σ min(1, p_k(x_k)/q_k(x_k))`, where `q` is the generation distribution and `p` is the model's introspective distribution.

- AR models: α = 1.0 (by construction; causal masking enforces it)
- Standard DLMs: α = 0.57–0.70 (trained to generate but not to endorse their outputs)
- I-DLM: trained to achieve high α while maintaining parallel generation

## I-DLM Training Recipe

Converts a pretrained AR model to a DLM via:
1. **Causal attention retained:** Unlike standard DLMs (bidirectional), I-DLM uses causal attention throughout
2. **Logit shift:** Adapts the prediction mechanism for multi-token diffusion while maintaining consistency
3. **All-masked objective:** Training with all tokens masked — but with AR-inherited causal structure
4. This makes I-DLM fully compatible with AR serving infrastructure (SGLang, paged KV cache)

## Introspective Strided Decoding (ISD)

Novel decoding algorithm:
- Generates N tokens per forward pass (parallel, diffusion-style)
- Simultaneously verifies prior tokens against a causal anchor distribution
- Combines parallel generation speed with AR-quality acceptance guarantees

## Results (I-DLM-8B)

- **MATH-500:** Matches Qwen3-8B (thinking) AR performance; +11.8pp over LLaDA-2.1-mini (16B)
- **AIME-24:** 69.6 (exceeds LLaDA-2.1-mini by >26 points)
- **LiveCodeBench-v6:** 45.7 (exceeds LLaDA-2.1-mini by >15 points)
- **Throughput (concurrency=32):** 3.1× over LLaDA-2.1-mini; 4.0× over SDAR-8B
- **~3× higher throughput** than prior SOTA DLMs at large-concurrency serving
