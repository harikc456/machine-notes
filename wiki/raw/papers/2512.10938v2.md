# DeepSeek-V3.2: Pushing the Frontier of Open Large Language Models

**Authors:** DeepSeek-AI  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, December 2025  
**arXiv:** 2512.10938

## Summary

DeepSeek-V3.2 introduces three key technical breakthroughs to advance open-source LLMs: DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA), a scalable RL framework for reasoning, and a large-scale agentic task synthesis pipeline.

## Key Technical Contributions

### 1. DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA)
An efficient attention mechanism that substantially reduces computational complexity in long-context scenarios while preserving performance. Addresses the efficiency bottleneck of vanilla attention for frontier models.

### 2. Scalable Reinforcement Learning Framework
- Allocates post-training compute exceeding 10% of pre-training budget
- Enables reasoning performance comparable to GPT-5 (standard variant)
- DeepSeek-V3.2-Speciale (high-compute variant) achieves parity with Gemini-3.0-Pro
- **Gold-medal performance** in IMO 2025, IOI 2025, ICPC World Final 2025, CMO 2025

### 3. Large-Scale Agentic Task Synthesis Pipeline
- Generated 1,800+ distinct environments and 85,000+ complex prompts
- Cold-start phase unifies reasoning and tool-use in single trajectories (building on DeepSeek-V3)
- Drives scalable agentic post-training; significant gains in generalization and instruction-following in interactive environments

## Results

- Reasoning: comparable to Kimi-k2-thinking and GPT-5 across multiple benchmarks
- Agentic: significantly advances open-source agentic capabilities (EvalSys, long-tail tasks)
- Substantially narrows gap between open and proprietary frontier models at lower cost

## Relationship to Prior DeepSeek Models

- Builds on DeepSeek-V3 (2024; MLA, DeepSeekMoE, multi-token prediction)
- DeepSeek-V4 report covers Pro (1.6T) and Flash (284B): CSA/HCA hybrid attention, mHC, Muon optimizer, 1M-token context
- V3.2 focuses on efficiency (DSA), reasoning (scalable RL), and agentic capability
