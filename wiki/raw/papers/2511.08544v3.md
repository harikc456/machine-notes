# LeJEPA: Provable and Scalable Self-Supervised Learning Without the Heuristics

**Authors:** Randall Balestriero, Yann LeCun  
**Affiliation:** Brown University, New York University (NYU), Meta-FAIR  
**Venue:** arXiv preprint, November 2025  
**arXiv:** 2511.08544

## Summary

LeJEPA provides a comprehensive theory of Joint Embedding Predictive Architectures (JEPAs) and instantiates it in a lean, scalable system. It proves that the isotropic Gaussian is the optimal embedding distribution for JEPAs and introduces SIGReg (Sketched Isotropic Gaussian Regularization) to provably enforce this distribution, eliminating all training heuristics.

## Core Contributions

### 1. Optimal Embedding Distribution (Theory)
Proves that the isotropic Gaussian uniquely minimizes downstream prediction risk for JEPAs — for both linear (Section 3.1) and nonlinear (Section 3.2) probes. This answers what distribution `f_θ` should produce, replacing empirical heuristics with a theoretical optimum.

### 2. SIGReg Objective
- **Mechanism:** Distribution matching via random projections + characteristic-function matching
- Enforces distributional alignment toward isotropic Gaussian
- Linear memory and computational complexity (in dimension and sample size)
- Bounded gradients; defeats the curse of dimensionality via projection-based construction
- Single trade-off hyperparameter `λ`

### 3. LeJEPA System
Combines JEPA predictive loss + SIGReg. Properties:
- No stop-gradients, teacher-student networks, EMA schedules, whitening layers
- No hyper-parameter schedulers
- Single hyperparameter (`λ`)
- Distributed training-friendly (~50 lines of code)
- Stable across models from 1.8B-g scale

## Results

- **79%** on ImageNet-1K with frozen ViT-H/14 backbone (1-shot)
- 10+ datasets, 60+ architectures tested; consistent improvements
- Domain-specific in-domain pretraining beats frontier models (DINOv2/v3) in transfer scenarios
- Loss strongly correlated with downstream accuracy (94.52% Spearman correlation on ViT-base) — enables model selection without supervised probing
