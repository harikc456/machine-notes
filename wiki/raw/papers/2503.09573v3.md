# Block Diffusion: Interpolating Between Autoregressive and Diffusion Language Models

**Authors:** Marianne Arriola, Aaron Kerem Gokaslan, Justin T. Chiu, Zhihan Yang, Zhixuan Qi, Jiaqi Han, Subham Sekhar Sahoo, Volodymyr Kuleshov  
**Affiliation:** Cornell Tech, Stanford University, Cohere  
**Venue:** ICLR 2025  
**arXiv:** 2503.09573

## Summary

Introduces Block Discrete Denoising Diffusion Language Models (BD3-LMs), a class of models that interpolates between autoregressive (AR) and diffusion language models. BD3-LMs define an AR distribution over *blocks* of tokens, with within-block generation handled by discrete denoising diffusion. This overcomes key limitations of both paradigms.

## Limitations Addressed

| Limitation | AR | Diffusion | BD3-LM |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fixed-length generation | ✅ No limit | ❌ Fixed-length | ✅ Arbitrary-length |
| KV caching | ✅ Yes | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Parallel token generation | ❌ No | ✅ Yes | ✅ Within blocks |
| Generation quality | ✅ High | ❌ Lower | ✅ SOTA among DLMs |

## Key Technical Contributions

1. **Block-level AR structure:** Tokens are divided into fixed-size blocks; each block is conditioned autoregressively on all preceding blocks → supports KV caching across blocks.

2. **Gradient variance analysis:** Shows that diffusion training has much higher gradient variance than AR training, even when the objectives are equivalent in expectation (block size = 1). This is a key root cause of the AR-diffusion quality gap — not architectural limitations.

3. **Data-driven noise schedules:** Custom noise schedules that minimize gradient variance, closing the perplexity gap substantially.

4. **Efficient training algorithm:** Leverages the full batch of tokens within a block for training without redundant forward passes.

## Results

- SOTA perplexity among discrete diffusion models on LM1B
- Generates sequences of arbitrary length (including beyond training context)
- Enables parallel token sampling within blocks for inference efficiency
